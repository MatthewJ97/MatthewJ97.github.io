WelcomeToWonderlandVR

A VR challenge where you need to use your wits and puzzle solving skills to survive and escape the galaxy's most dangerous game show and stay one step ahead of its charismatic, crazed, omnipresent host. Welcome to Wonderland.

Welcome to Wonderland was created as my final project for my Advanced Diploma of Game Development (Programming)

Tasks

Programming Contributions:

  • NVR Integration/Modification: Due to the games size/scope, we didn’t want to spend much time setting up a big system that is already available for is for free. Modifications were made to the teleporting and other elements to better suit the needs we had for prototyping quickly.

  • Voiceline Management System: Created a few simple function inside of Joel Gabriel’s Voiceline Management system to better suit the purposes of my other systems. Modified some the playvoiceline script to no play clips over one another. Play voiceline delayed waits a specific amount of time and plays a desired voiceline.

  • AI Pathing Behaviours: Created a simple ai that “wanders” through the scene with no set path or destination giving the impression of a defective robot or a waiting robot.This was then used to make a waiter bot within the “cinema candy bar”.

  • Time Manager: Due to the Timed nature of our game a Time manager was need to correctly initiate states based on what the player was doing at certain times. mainly created for the beta where the player had 10 minutes to setup the “stage” for the Antagonist.

  • Teleporting Whilst holding mouth objects: Created a simple system that allows the player to teleport whilst holding objects in there mouth. This allows for player accessories their avatar and teleport without removing them from their avatar

  • Smoking cigars and consumables: Created a system where the player can smoke cigars, cigarettes and consume items. the system relies on a trigger on the head which picks up objects and reacts to ones of consumable nature.

  • Object Highlighting: Implemented an asset that allowed for the player to see what object their hand is in when trying to pick up objects and then reconfigured it to work with our objects and the player.

  • Puzzle Handler: A system that was designed to take care of each puzzle in the project, the script is stacks onto a gameobject in unity to centralise all puzzles. The script checks the puzzles completed state every frame. This is then linked into each puzzle which handles its own puzzle completion state

  • Puzzle Logic: The puzzle logic was a abstract class that each puzzle inherits from. it sets the base functions and variables needed to be classified as a puzzle. Each puzzle was then created to use this structure allowing for the puzzle handler to control the results of completion and allow for play testing to have debug bypasses.

    • Prototype Puzzles:
      •
      • Stage Lights Puzzle: This puzzle involved the player selecting the correct sequence of lights for the stage to open and allow for passage. The puzzle was also linked to a puzzle sequence handler that would flash lights in the correct sequence (created by Joel Gabriel).
      • Eye scanner: A simple puzzle where the player had to take a photo of a picture and scan it into an eye scanner to pass through.

    • Gold Puzzles:
      •
      • Cinema Seat Puzzle: A puzzle that involves the player placing objects on top of buttons in a pattern to unlock the stage.
      • Quiz Show Puzzle: A simple quiz that the player must complete to pass through the level. Each question that they get wrong suffers a time penalty.
